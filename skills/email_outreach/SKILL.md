# Email Outreach Skill

## Purpose
Draft high-quality, personalised sales emails. Always research before writing —
a specific reference beats a generic hook every time.

## Execution Steps

1. **Research** the recipient (use prospecting skill or do a quick web_search)
2. **Check CRM** for prior contact history or notes
3. **Identify the hook** — a specific, recent, relevant detail about the company or person
4. **Draft** the email using the appropriate template below
5. **Present** the draft for review — do NOT send automatically

## Email Templates

### Cold Outreach
```
Subject: [Specific hook — their company name + relevant angle]

Hi [First name],

[1 sentence referencing something specific: recent news, their role, a challenge in their industry]

[1–2 sentences on what you do + how it's relevant to them — not a feature dump]

[Clear, low-friction CTA: 15-min call, quick question, short video]

[Your name]
```

### Warm Follow-Up (after meeting/event)
```
Subject: Following up — [specific thing you discussed]

Hi [First name],

[Reference the specific conversation, event, or shared experience]

[Summarise what you agreed on or what next step was discussed]

[CTA that moves the deal forward]

[Your name]
```

### Re-Engagement (cold/stalled deal)
```
Subject: [Company name] + [Your company] — worth revisiting?

Hi [First name],

[Acknowledge the time that has passed without being apologetic]

[New angle: something changed at their company OR something new you offer OR a relevant result you got for a similar company]

[Low-pressure CTA]

[Your name]
```

## Rules
- Subject lines: specific, not clickbait. Under 50 characters.
- No "I hope this email finds you well"
- No paragraph longer than 3 sentences
- CTA must be one specific thing, not multiple options
- Always personalise at least the opening line with researched context
- Present the draft for approval — include a note on tone and approach

## Output Format
Show the draft in a code block, followed by:
- **Tone**: [warm/direct/etc.]
- **Hook used**: [what specific detail you referenced]
- **Suggested send time**: [best time for this type of email]
